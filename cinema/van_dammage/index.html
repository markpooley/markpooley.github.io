From Drew: As a kid, I watched more action flicks than children's movies. My mom rented all the latest popcorn fares from the likes of Segal, Van Damme, Stallone, and others. I often considered Under Siege, Bloodsport, and Hard Target to be the cream of the crop, so I was surprised to discover my recollection of the latter was completely inaccurate. I remembered Hard Target as though it was Surviving the Game, but with JCVD replacing Ice-T in the role of the hunted.

This revelation was actually a pleasant surprise, however. Van Damme was less vagrant in the woods being pursued sport, and more of a badass engaging in urban warfare with a truly despicable group of baddies.

And actually, my favorite aspect of Hard Target is its villains. Led by Lance Henriksen, and the dude from The Mummy, the bad guys are classic action-movie evil. While they're all one-note, static characters, most of whom are simply cannon fodder, their evil-doings alone make them fun to root against. I mean, hunting homeless people for sport and profit? Short of punching babies or drowning puppies, these guys are cartoonishly villainous. Ultimately, it's a lot of fun watching them grow increasingly frustrated at Van Damme's antics. They're the kind of characters you like to see earn their just deserts.

With so many disposable villains, there are some pretty fun set pieces built in to take them out. Van Damme provides his signature moves, spin kicking the enemies from motorcycles before eventually surfing on a chopper his own. He even punches a rattlesnake, which alone makes the movie memorable.

The movie really excels when Wilford Brimley, offers his assistance. Playing Van Damme's backwoods survivalist uncle, Brimley's character tilts the playing field, making the odds completely in favor of our heroes.

The final chase sequence cumulates in a showdown in a warehouse for Mardi Gras floats, which, is different. I would have liked seeing more use of this location and it's props but ultimately it feels tacked on to simply reinforce the movie is set in New Orleans.

Adding John Woo and Sam Raimi to the mix, and the movie had a lot of weight and talent behind it. As a result, the movie holds up better than expected. Hard Target is still definitely a product of its time but still very enjoyable for fans of the genre. Oh, and Van Damme rocks a mullet, which is pretty sweet. It really informs his character.

From Mark: I love this movie. I used to own it on VHS and watched the hell out of it. JCVD pulls no punches on being a truly masterful action hero (including punching out the aforementioned snake). He's no match for a horde of rich white dudes looking to kill another white dude. He's a cunning detective, a mastered martial artist, and a true lady killer to boot..

Like Drew, I revere this iconic JCVD flick as one his best. Even 20 years after it's release it holds up perfectly. Denim, mullets, Camero Z-28, Blues slide guitar - it's a magnificent piece of American action cinema. I wanted more splits and nut punches. But, a viewer can only handle so much badassery (of which this story was full).

General

This is a magnificent piece of American cinema. John Woo takes action to the next level with the best martial artist in the bayou harboring a Belgian accent. The ode to old westerns, music, triple slow-motion takes, explosions, and gun slinging all add up to one masterful action movie. Have you ever seen a guy punch out a rattlesnake? JC has a body count of 34 (including the snake).

Sam Raimi, that Sam Raimi, oversaw production in order to quell studio concerns about Woo's limited English. Thank God Raimi was involved, you know that guy added his own flair to the production!
